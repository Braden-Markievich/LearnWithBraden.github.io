Limit Comparison Test

Another way to determine if a series converges or diverges is with the limit comparison test. With this test, you create a series that you can quickly evaluate that is very similar to the one provided, but it's either greater or less than the provided one, given that both series are positive. Once you have created a new series, take the limit of the original series and divide it by the new series. If the final answer for the limit is not equal to zero and finite, then the two series will perform the same, so you can evaluate the new series and it will be the same answer for the original series. Examples are provided below.

Steps:
1. Create a similar series to the original series
2. Take the limit of the original series divided by the new series
3. If limit is finite and non-zero, the two series act the same, so you can evaluate the new series.


Question 1: \[\sum _{n=2}^{\infty }\:{1 \over n^3 -1}\]

Question 2: \[\sum _{n=2}^{\infty }\:{{n^3 + 14n} \over 4n^4 +12n^2}\]

Question 3: \[\sum _{n=2}^{\infty }\:{1 \over n^{1/2}+4}\]
